Olive Tree Campus Church Website - Community Love

Olive Tree Campus Church is a Chinese student church based in Ann Arbor, Michigan aiming to serve students and scholars at the University of Michigan and the local community. Established in 2012, Olive Tree Campus Church is very young and unique, trying to help more students better understand belief. As a member of the church, I volunteered to be the designer and developer for the website of the church and successfully launched the website in December, 2016.


Problem - Why do I need to develop the website?

Initially all the activities were announced within our social network accounts. However, as the church community grows larger, there is an increasing need in broadening our influence in the community and providing better access for people who want to visit our church. I made the aaotcc.org website as a central hub for our regular services and events, stories & photos of past events, and a contact form for general questions or inquiries.

Concept - An ideal organization website should have...

Personas - Who are the audience(s) I am targeting?

Ideally, every person, no matter a Christian or a general visitor who is interested to visit a local church is my target user. Though Olive Tree Campus Church is based in University of Michigan campus mainly aiming students, scholars and young professionals, regular visitors or new-comers can have a wide range of needs for information on the website. In this case, I conducted interviews with 5 people who have visited Olive Tree Campus Church before and are illustrative for the diverse group. I then created personas to reflect their needs and attitudes towards a church website.

Design Rationale - How can the website satisfy user needs?

A website of an organization is simply the best medium for people outside to know about the organization. It is not simplying putting up everything on the main page. Selecting the most useful information to present and differentiating from other general churches to leave audiences a good first-impression are the key leverage I considered when I designed the website.

Site Map and Wireframes - What should the website be like? How to arrange all the information?

With the design concept and the design rationale, I generated a set of site map and wireframes for the arrangement of UI components and iterations. The wireframes were used to quickly visualize a website layout.


Solution - Olive Tree Campus Church Responsive Website - Launched !

With Sketch and JustInMind, I was able to make the hi-fidelity prototype of the website. With the prototype, I then spent 2 months effort coding and testing the website. Now I am thrilled to announce that the website has been successfully launched and put in use. Below is the preview of the desktop screen.
