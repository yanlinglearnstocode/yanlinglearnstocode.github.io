TripAdvisor Intelligent Travel Support

Provide Smart Recommendations to make In-Destination Planning Convenient and Fun




Overview

The Project

Background: With over 450m monthly users and 500m reviews, TripAdvisor is the largest travel community in the world. TripAdvisor has set a goal to be a "travel sidekick" maximizing the full potential of a trip. In the last couple of years, TripAdvisor has been evolving its solutions from a find modality where a user actively looks for information to a discover experience where user comes to the site with softer intent and rely on TripAdvisor to assist the traveler in discovering.

Proposed Solution: A unique recommendation system embedded in the TripAdvisor mobile application that acknowledges different travel intents and enhances travelers' in-destination experience.

Client: TripAdvisor

Timeline: Jan 2018 - April 2018



The Challenge

Given the huge travel-centric dataset for TripAdvisor and entrenched app and web usage, create a more assistive and personalized experience for travelers when they are on the trip

The Context

Opportunity in the Travel Market Landscape

With over 450m monthly users and 500m reviews, TripAdvisor is the largest travel community in the world. According to the article "Meet your lucrative millennial travler" posted on the TripAdvisor website, millennials place utmost importance on tourist activities and online reviews in their decision making process of where to travel (TripAdvisor, 2017). Research conducted by Google's VP of engineering for travel and shopping in 2017 suggests that "57% of US travelers feel that brands should tailor its information and overall trip".

The Research

User Research

After the initial meeting with TripAdvisor's Product and UX Research team, we had an initial understanding about the context. In order to further define the problem and to identify the design direction. We kicked off our own research with 12 people to understand how people decide to go on trips, how they plan their trips and what roadblocks they encounter along the way. (Participants are aged 20 - 35, with diverse occupations and travel experiences)

One interesting observation we found from our research was differnt travlers have different travel personalities and intents for the trips. These varying personalities and intents cause conflicts during group travel, making the planning process especially harder.

Based on our user research, we generated a user persona with information about their behaviors, travel habits, pain points and their needs. Meet our persona Zoey:



Problem Space

Opportunities Arised from the Research

1. There is much stress and effort arised in a group trip with constant decision making.

2. There is a gradual shift from users actively looking for information to relying on expert assistance and embracing the experience of discovering.

3. Currently TripAdvisor has an "Invite friends" feature that allows users to collaborate on creating itinerary that has not been fully utilized.

Define the goal

Reframe the problem

How can we redesign TripAdvisor mobile app to create a more assistive experience for the traveler and to reduce the traveler's stress of constant decision making on a trip?

System Architecture

How does the unique recommendation system work

In our proposed solution, we designed a unique trip recommendation mechanism that adopts a system architecture as displayed in the graph. The system consumes data from multiple ends, including general user data, trip-specific data, TripAdvisor community data, and potential external utility data (weather, map, events calendar, etc.), all of which could form a giant "travel data" network that is able to constantly grow.


The Solution

Put together the workflow

After three rounds of iterations to the design, I created an initail set of prototypes to illustrate the idea and the workflow. The solution is an amalgamation of user needs, client goals, identified gap in the market and TripAdvisor's strength with a focus of the persona.

Design Details

Input Flexibility

In addition to basic travel information like destination and dates, users now can input their intent and needs with guidance or freely with natural language to help the application better understand users' intent for the trip.

Contextual Recommendation & Feedback Iteration

The system comes up with recommendation based on user input. "Best match" represents the results that accommodate as many requests as possible. The "heart" icon is designed for users to save events to their itinerary. The "refresh" icon is designed for users to switch new options. Both actions allow users to quickly provide feedback to the system so that it can keep learning what users are interested in.

A screen with detailed event/place information will pop up when user taps the card for more info. It provides users with reasons why the system recommends the event. It bonds a closer relationship to the user, and a user can feel that the system is really thinking for them.

Trip Itinerary

A specific itinerary page is designed to keep track of all the trip details for users. The itinerary page helps user keep track of the entire trip. It also archived critical documents that are related with the trip, for example, tickets, reservation receipts, bills, etc.



Conclusion

Reflection & Takeaways

We designed this solution keeping in mind TripAdvisor's competitive advantage. As research has shown millennial travelers are on the rise, who, are seeking tailored travel suggestions. This intelligent approach optimally leverages TripAdvisor's assets without changing who they are as a brand. As an AI-powered solution, our customized recommendation mechanism connects TripAdvisor and travelers in a more engaging way by better understanding travelers' intent and providing contextual options.

However, we also identified potential limitations of adopting AI-powered recommendation systems including: the challenge of handling constantly changing intents, the challenge of gaining user trust, and the challenge of balancing the serendipity and user expectations.
